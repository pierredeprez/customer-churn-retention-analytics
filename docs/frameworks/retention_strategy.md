# Retention Strategy Framework — Revenue-Weighted Decision Support

## Purpose
This document translates churn and revenue analytics into a **practical retention strategy**.  
It defines *who to prioritize*, *when to intervene*, and *why resources should be allocated based on financial impact rather than customer volume*.

The framework is designed for **executive decision-making**, not operational automation.

---

## Strategic Principle
Not all churn is equally harmful.

Retention efforts should focus on:
- Customers who are **still active**
- Customers with **material revenue impact**
- Customers whose churn is **preventable through timely intervention**

This project therefore prioritizes **revenue at risk**, not raw churn counts.

---

## Retention Prioritization Dimensions

Customers are evaluated along two independent dimensions:

### 1. Churn Risk (Likelihood)
A forward-looking signal indicating probability of disengagement, informed by:
- Contract structure
- Tenure
- Service mix
- Billing and support signals

Churn risk is treated as a **decision support signal**, not an automated trigger.

---

### 2. Revenue Impact (Magnitude)
The financial consequence if churn occurs, measured using:
- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- Current billing exposure

Revenue impact is assessed **before** applying churn probability to avoid over-prioritizing low-value churn.

---

## Retention Segmentation Matrix

| Segment | Risk Level | Revenue Impact | Strategic Action |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Tier 1 | High | High | Immediate intervention |
| Tier 2 | Medium | High | Preventive stabilization |
| Tier 3 | High | Low | Tactical / low-cost action |
| Tier 4 | Low | Low | Monitor only |

This matrix ensures that:
- High-value customers are protected first
- Resources are not consumed by low-impact churn

---

## Strategic Retention Focus

### Primary Focus: Medium-Risk, High-Revenue Customers
Although high-risk customers require urgency, **medium-risk customers represent the largest preventable revenue pool**.

They are:
- Still behaviorally engaged
- Less operationally disengaged
- More responsive to retention actions

This makes them the **highest ROI retention segment**.

---

### Secondary Focus: Early-Tenure Customers (≤12 months)
Churn risk and behavioral volatility are highest early in the customer lifecycle.

Retention investment is most effective when:
- Intervening before habits solidify
- Reinforcing onboarding and service understanding
- Stabilizing billing expectations early

---

## Recommended Retention Levers

Based on observed churn drivers:

- Incentivize longer-term contracts
- Proactively engage month-to-month customers
- Improve service reliability for internet-dependent customers
- Clarify billing and payment processes
- Address unresolved technical issues early

These levers are **directional strategy inputs**, not prescriptive campaign rules.

---

## Guardrails & Assumptions

- No causal inference is claimed
- No uplift modeling is performed
- Retention impact is assumed uniform across customers
- Strategy is designed for **prioritization**, not automation

All recommendations must be validated through controlled experiments before execution.

---

## Strategic Outcome

This framework enables leadership to:
- Allocate retention budgets efficiently
- Defend retention investments financially
- Shift churn management from reactive to preventive

Retention success is maximized by **acting earlier in the risk curve**, not by reacting after churn becomes inevitable.
