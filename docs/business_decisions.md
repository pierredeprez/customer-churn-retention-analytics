# Executive Decision Memo — Retention Strategy

## Purpose
This memo translates analytical findings into **explicit business decisions**
that leadership could act on immediately.

---

## Key Findings Recap
- Monthly revenue exposure is structurally concentrated among active customers
- Medium-risk customers account for the largest share of preventable revenue
- Early-tenure customers represent the highest ROI intervention window
- High-risk customers require tactical, time-sensitive action

---

## Strategic Decisions

### 1. Retention Investment Prioritization
**Decision:**  
Allocate the majority of retention budget toward **medium-risk, high-revenue**
customers rather than only imminent churn cases.

**Rationale:**  
These customers remain behaviorally engaged and offer the highest potential
return on retention spend.

---

### 2. Early-Tenure Stabilization
**Decision:**  
Introduce onboarding and engagement reinforcements during the first 6–12 months.

**Rationale:**  
Churn probability declines significantly after early tenure stabilization.

---

### 3. Tactical High-Risk Intervention
**Decision:**  
Deploy targeted outreach for high-risk customers with above-average monthly
charges.

**Rationale:**  
These customers represent urgent, short-term revenue exposure.

---

## What This Analysis Does NOT Decide
- No pricing changes are recommended
- No automated churn actions are triggered
- No causal claims are made

This ensures responsible, decision-support use of analytics.

---

## Bottom Line
Retention value is maximized by **acting earlier in the risk curve**, not by
reacting after churn becomes inevitable.
